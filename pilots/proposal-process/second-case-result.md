# Second Case Result

## Executive summary

- Problem: repetitive manual responses and unclear information ownership.
- Recommended choice: begin with one limited information scope.
- Reason: the input, output, and review responsibility can be verified quickly.
- Main risks: unclear ownership, outdated content, and continued reliance on manual channels.

## Minimum viable scope

- Input: reviewed information from one narrow service area.
- Output: consistent answers, source references, and escalation when the system cannot answer.
- Excluded: full organizational knowledge, transactions, and automated decisions.

## Six-page slide outline

1. The problem is fragmented information ownership, not a lack of AI tools.
2. Stakeholders have different adoption incentives and objections.
3. Start with one limited information scope and a reviewable workflow.
4. Define feature, data, ownership, and escalation boundaries.
5. Separate claims, evidence, assumptions, and missing data.
6. Confirm decisions, risks, actions, and the next validation stage.
