# Contextual Re-identification Checklist

Automated scanning detects direct patterns. It does not determine whether a combination of ordinary details can reveal the original organization, project, person, or event.

Complete this checklist before publishing a generated Skill, example, pilot result, or maintenance note.

## Combined identifiers

- [ ] Industry and niche service details are generalized.
- [ ] Exact dates, quarters, and event sequences are generalized.
- [ ] Exact values, volumes, percentages, and price ranges are removed or replaced.
- [ ] Locations and uncommon operating areas are generalized.
- [ ] Job titles and relationship descriptions cannot identify a specific person.
- [ ] Product names, internal abbreviations, and uncommon workflow terms are removed.
- [ ] The combination of stakeholders, technology, and process does not reveal the source.

## Source reconstruction

- [ ] A reader cannot reconstruct the original meeting, proposal, or project timeline.
- [ ] Search engines cannot connect unusual phrases to the source document.
- [ ] The example does not preserve a distinctive sequence of decisions or objections.
- [ ] The remaining information is a reusable method rather than a disguised case summary.

## Publication decision

Stop publication when any reviewer can reasonably infer the original source context.

Automated scan status and human review status must be recorded separately.
